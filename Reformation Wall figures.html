Understanding the Reformation Wall


The Reformation Wall was completed in 1909 in the Swiss city of Geneva. It depicts ten figures from Reformation history. I need you to consider what sort of history this monument was trying to display.

While the Wall was built in Geneva, the figures portrayed in its stone were from countries far outside of Switzerland. This map shows the locations of these people, colour coded by where they appear on the Wall. You can also see the icon of the statue, showing the whereabouts of the Reformation Wall itself.

Why did the Wall depict so many different figures from outside of Switzerland? What were its designers trying to achieve?

You can watch this short video to get an idea of the scale of the wall, as well as some of the ideas behind its creation.
